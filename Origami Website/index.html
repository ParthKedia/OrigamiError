Facts About Camel:
  • Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Facts About Chameleon:
  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  • Chameleons have good eyesight for a lizard
  • Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long


Facts About Pigeon:
  • Pigeons understand space and time.
  • Pigeons saved many people during World Wars I and II.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.


Facts About Bears:
  • Bears are intelligent.
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  • Bears care deeply about family members.


Facts About Panda:
  • Pandas have eyes different to normal bears.
  • Pandas spend most of the day eating.
  • Pandas occasionally eat something other than bamboo even though its critical to their diet.


Facts About Cicadas:
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Most Cicadas have red-orange eyes.
  • Cicadas have one of the longest lifespans for bugs.
